In Boolean algebra, operations are performed in a specific order:

Order of Operations

  1. Brackets - Always first
  2. NOT - Highest precedence
  3. AND - Middle precedence
  4. OR - Lowest precedence

üîÑ Commutative & Associative Laws

Commutative Law

The order of operands doesn't matter:

  • A . B = B . A
  • A + B = B + A

Associative Law

Grouping doesn't matter when all operations are the same:

  • (A . B) . C = A . (B . C)
  • (A + B) + C = A + (B + C)

üìê Distributive Law

The distributive law allows us to expand or factorize Boolean expressions:

Distributive Rules

  • A . (B + C) = A.B + A.C
  • A + (B . C) = (A + B) . (A + C)

üßΩ Absorption Laws

Absorption laws eliminate redundant terms in Boolean expressions:

Key Absorption Laws

  • A + A.B = A
  • A.(A + B) = A
  • A.(A + B) = A.B
  • A + A.B = A + B

True or False: Absorption Laws

1. X + X.Y simplifies to X

2. X.(X + Y) simplifies to X.Y

3. A + A.B simplifies to A + B

Mark Scheme:
  • Factor out common terms: A.(B + B) + A.B (1 mark)
