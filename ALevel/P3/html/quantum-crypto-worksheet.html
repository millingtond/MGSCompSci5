⚛️ Quantum Fundamentals

What is Quantum Cryptography?

Quantum cryptography uses quantum mechanical properties to secure communication. Unlike classical cryptography that relies on mathematical complexity, quantum cryptography's security is guaranteed by the laws of physics.

🔑 Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)

Quantum Key Distribution allows two parties to produce a shared random secret key using quantum mechanics. The most famous protocol is BB84.

BB84 Protocol Simulation

Step through the BB84 quantum key distribution protocol:

Step 1: Alice prepares qubits

Alice randomly chooses bits (0 or 1) and basis (rectilinear | or diagonal ×)

Step 2: Alice sends qubits

Qubits are sent through a quantum channel (optical fiber)

Step 3: Bob measures

Bob randomly chooses measurement basis for each qubit

Step 4: Basis comparison

Alice and Bob publicly compare bases (not values!)

Step 5: Key generation

They keep bits where bases matched, discard others

Benefit 2: Key Integrity

Why can't quantum keys be copied?

No-Cloning Theorem: It is impossible to create an identical copy of an arbitrary unknown quantum state. This means: - Keys cannot be duplicated without detection - Perfect eavesdropping is impossible - Key integrity is guaranteed by physics
