Question 1 (4 marks)

Explain the difference between CSMA/CA and why it is necessary in wireless networks compared to wired networks.

Mark Scheme:

  • CSMA/CA prevents collisions before they happen (1 mark)
  • Necessary because wireless uses broadcast transmission (1 mark)
  • All devices share the same medium/frequency (1 mark)
  • Devices listen before transmitting to avoid interference (1 mark)

Question 2 (6 marks)

Describe the complete process of sending data wirelessly from one device to another, including all necessary steps.

Mark Scheme:

  • Data is encrypted using TKIP or AES (1 mark)
  • Data is encoded into radio/analogue signal (1 mark)
  • Device listens for other transmissions (1 mark)
  • Transmits when channel is clear (1 mark)
  • Uses antenna/WNIC/WAP to broadcast (1 mark)
  • Receiving device decodes and decrypts (1 mark)

📊 Summary & Assessment

Key Takeaways

  • Wireless transmission uses radio, microwave, or infrared waves
  • The main difference is the frequency of the waves
  • Bluetooth uses low frequency radio waves
  • CSMA/CA prevents collisions in wireless networks
  • Key hardware: WAP, WNIC, Router, Repeater
  • Wireless offers mobility but wired offers better speed/security

Common Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️

Mixing up encryption and encoding: Encryption is for security, encoding is for transmission format

⚠️

Confusing WAP with router: WAP connects wireless to wired, router connects different networks

⚠️

Thinking wireless is always better: Consider the trade-offs for each situation
