Common Applications

Touch screens are everywhere! Here are some common uses:

  • Mobile phones/tablets: Primarily capacitive for multi-touch gestures
  • ATMs: Often resistive for reliability and glove compatibility
  • Restaurant POS systems: Capacitive for speed and durability
  • Industrial controls: Resistive for harsh environments
  • Car infotainment: Capacitive for modern interface experience

üìù Summary & Final Quiz

Key Takeaways

  • Speakers: Convert electrical signals to sound via electromagnetic fields
  • Microphones: Convert sound to electrical signals (opposite of speakers)
  • DAC/ADC: Essential for converting between digital and analog
  • Resistive screens: Pressure-based, work with any input
  • Capacitive screens: Charge-based, support multi-touch
  • Applications: Choose based on environment and requirements

Question 2 (4 marks)

Compare resistive and capacitive touch screens. Give two advantages of each type.

Mark Scheme (1 mark per valid advantage, max 2 per type):

Resistive advantages:

  • Works with any input (finger, glove, stylus)
  • Relatively inexpensive technology
  • Precise input possible

Capacitive advantages:

  • Supports multi-touch gestures
  • More durable (glass surface)
  • Better visibility in sunlight
  • More responsive/sensitive
