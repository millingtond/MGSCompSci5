üìù Summary & Final Quiz

Key Takeaways

  • Binary addition follows specific rules with carry operations
  • Overflow occurs when results exceed available bits
  • One's complement inverts all bits
  • Two's complement = one's complement + 1
  • Subtraction uses two's complement addition
  • Signed numbers use MSB as sign bit

üìã Exam Practice Questions

Question 1 (6 marks)

a) Perform the following binary addition: 10110111 + 01101101 (2 marks)

b) Convert -45 to 8-bit two's complement representation. Show all working. (3 marks)

c) Explain why overflow cannot occur when adding a positive and negative number in two's complement. (1 mark)

Mark Scheme:

a) Binary addition (2 marks):

  • Correct working shown with carries: 1 mark
  • Correct answer: 100100100 - 1 mark

b) Two's complement conversion (3 marks):

  • Positive binary of 45: 00101101 - 1 mark
  • One's complement: 11010010 - 1 mark
  • Two's complement: 11010011 - 1 mark

c) Explanation (1 mark):

  • When adding positive and negative numbers, the result is always within the representable range - 1 mark

Question 2 (4 marks)

A computer uses 8-bit two's complement representation.

a) State the range of integers that can be represented. (1 mark)

b) Show how the calculation 67 - 29 would be performed using two's complement. (3 marks)

Mark Scheme:

a) Range (1 mark):

  • -128 to 127 - 1 mark

b) Subtraction calculation (3 marks):

  • 67 in binary: 01000011 - 0.5 marks
  • 29 in binary: 00011101 - 0.5 marks
  • Two's complement of 29: 11100011 - 1 mark
  • Addition and result: 00100110 (38 in decimal) - 1 mark
