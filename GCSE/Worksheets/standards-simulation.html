Technology Standards

How Standards Enable Global Technology Compatibility

What Are Technology Standards?

A standard is a published document that specifies how hardware or software must behave or interact.

Standards ensure that products from different manufacturers can work together seamlessly.

Key Characteristics of Standards

📄

Published Documents

Official specifications that anyone can follow

🤝

Agreed Rules

Industry-wide agreements on how things work

🌍

Global Adoption

Used worldwide by all manufacturers

🔧

Technical Details

Precise specifications for implementation

Without Standards...

Imagine if every phone manufacturer used different charging cables, or if websites looked different in every browser, or if you couldn't connect devices from different brands!

Common Technology Standards

Click on each standard to learn more!

📶

Wi-Fi (802.11)

Wireless networking standard

Enables wireless communication between devices. Different versions: 802.11n, 802.11ac, 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)

🌐

HTML

Web page structure standard

Defines how web pages are structured. Current version: HTML5. Ensures websites work in all browsers.

🔌

Cat 5e Cable

Ethernet cable standard

Supports up to 1 Gbps speeds at 100 meters. Uses RJ45 connectors. Common in home/office networks.

⚡

Cat 6 Cable

Enhanced cable standard

Supports up to 10 Gbps at 55 meters. Better shielding than Cat 5e. Used for high-speed networks.

🔗

USB

Universal Serial Bus

Connects peripherals to computers. Versions: USB 2.0 (480 Mbps), USB 3.0 (5 Gbps), USB-C (reversible).

📡

Bluetooth

Short-range wireless

Connects devices wirelessly over short distances. Used in headphones, keyboards, fitness trackers.

Standards Organizations

IEEE - Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (Wi-Fi, Ethernet)

W3C - World Wide Web Consortium (HTML, CSS, Web standards)

ISO - International Organization for Standardization (Various standards)

ITU - International Telecommunication Union (Telecom standards)

Ethernet Evolution Timeline

1973 - Invention

Ethernet invented at Xerox PARC

1983 - IEEE 802.3

Became official standard (10 Mbps)

1995 - Fast Ethernet

100 Mbps standard released

1999 - Gigabit Ethernet

1 Gbps speeds achieved

Today - 100G+

100 Gbps and beyond
